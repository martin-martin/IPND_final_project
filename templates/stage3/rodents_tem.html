An Example

Now let's assume we are looking at the Order Rodents. Even though many animals that are part of it look very different from each other. But they share some characteristics (or a basic blueprint) that makes us group e.g. a mouse and a capybara together. This can entail both states (e.g. how their skull is constructed) as well as behaviours.
Somewhere high up the ancestral tree: they are both rodents.


Now, Rodents could be considered our Class, while mouses and capybara could be considered objects of that Class. And then, further down the line, our actual representations of the mouses and the single capybara that I drew here, would be the instances of this (simplified) Class.


There's so much messy discussion about this taxonomy out there in the world of biology, so my analogy is probably wrong on so many ends, that it's not even worth counting them... But it does make some things understandable for me in terms of programming.
